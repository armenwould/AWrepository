Misophonia

Misophonia is a rare psychological disorder that afflicts less than 200 000 people in the United States. Derived literally from Greek, "miso" is hatred of and "phonia" is sound. People afflicted with this disorder have a peculiar sensitivity to certain sounds. When exposed to these sounds, the most common of which are chewing, lip smacking, pencil tapping, and yawning, the afflictant usually experiences the following:

  • anxiety
  • discomfort
  • the urge to flee
  • disgust

Or, if the condition is more severe:

  • rage
  • anger
  • panic/panic attacks
  • fear
  • emotional distress
  • a desire to kill or stop that which is producing the sound
  • skin crawling
  • suicidal thoughts
